01 — Your starting position

You hold three advantages most people don't.

This is not motivation-poster talk. These are structural advantages in the exact job market you are aiming at.

A—

You are a US citizen

Being Puerto Rican means citizenship, and citizenship means you are eligible for a security clearance. Most people on Earth never can be. Cleared IT workers earn more and get hired faster, because the clearance is often harder to find than the skill.

B—

You are already on base

You work inside a US military installation. The IT offices there, government and contractor alike, hire from people who are already local, already badged, already known. You can walk to the buildings that will employ you.

C—

The degree line is negotiable

Department of Defense rules, known as DoD 8140, qualify people through certifications. Postings often add "bachelor's degree or equivalent experience," and in practice, certs plus a clearance plus time on the desk is the equivalent. If the paper matters later, WGU's online IT degrees give credit for the certificates in this plan while you work.

The cheat code

CompTIA Security+ is the key that opens the base doors.

Nearly every IT job that touches a DoD network legally requires a baseline certification, and Security+ is the standard one. Help desk at the network center, contractor field tech, sysadmin support: they all list it. This whole roadmap points at Security+ by the end of year one. Once you hold it and can clear, you sit in a small pool of people that government contractors compete to hire.

02 — The route

One year. Five gears.

The gears overlap: you will often be in two at once, and that is the point. The first one pays off in weeks, not months. The timeline assumes five to eight hours a week while you keep your job.

First gearWeeks 0–6 · The fast win

AI certificates now. Better job now.

AI certificates need zero IT background, and they upgrade the resume you already have, for the jobs you can already get. This gear is about more money and a systems-adjacent title while the bigger certs cook.

  • This weekend: Career Essentials in Generative AI by Microsoft and LinkedIn. Free, about five hours, a real certificate on your LinkedIn profile by Sunday night.
  • Follow with Google AI Essentials on Coursera, about $49, one to two weeks. Optional third: Microsoft's Azure AI-900 exam, $99, the first one a DoD resume screener recognizes.
  • Rebuild the resume around them, then apply up immediately: inventory, logistics, and admin roles on base, anywhere the person who actually knows AI tools wins the tiebreak. Ask HR about openings and education assistance in the same conversation.
  • In parallel, start Professor Messer's free A+ course on YouTube to see if the deeper IT world clicks.
Cost · $0–150
Second gearMonths 1–6

CompTIA A+, your first real IT certificate.

A+ proves you can support computers, printers, phones, and the people attached to them. It is the entry ticket for help desk work everywhere, and its second exam is mostly a Windows exam, so the Windows side of your OS knowledge gets certified here.

  • Two exams, Core 1 and Core 2, roughly $250+ each. Confirm current pricing at comptia.org.
  • Study with Messer, then drill practice exams. ExamCompass is free; Messer's paid sets are worth the money.
  • Start applying at month three, before you finish. On-base help desk, contractor entry roles, remote service desks. Not when you feel ready. Nobody ever feels ready.
Cost · ~$500 in exams
Third gearMonths 6–9 · The big one

CompTIA Security+, the DoD door opener.

The certificate this whole plan is built around, pulled forward because it opens doors and nothing else does. One exam, roughly $400, and it satisfies the DoD 8140 baseline that base IT jobs demand.

  • Study Messer's free Security+ course, then practice exams until you hold 85 percent or better.
  • The day you pass: open a USAJOBS.gov account, search IT roles at your installation, and check the career pages of GDIT, Leidos, Vectrus, and KBR, all of whom staff bases in Germany.
  • If a job offers to sponsor your security clearance, take it, even at average pay. The clearance is worth more than the first salary.
Cost · ~$400
Fourth gearMonths 9–12 · The combo

CompTIA Linux+, the OS certificate that counts.

Technical resumes in the DoD world carry two things: Security+ and an operating system certificate the postings recognize. Windows is already certified inside A+ Core 2. Linux+ covers the other half, the one that servers, security tooling, and the cloud run on.

  • One exam, roughly $370. Free courses on YouTube cover it; install Ubuntu in a virtual machine and live in the terminal while you study.
  • Straight talk: OS certificates are not part of the 8140 baseline, that is Security+'s job. They live in the job postings themselves, where Linux+ and Red Hat's RHCSA are the recognized names. Linux+ is the easier and cheaper of the two.
  • You can start applying with Security+ alone. Finish Linux+ while you work; employers respect a cert in progress.
Cost · ~$370
Fifth gearYear 2 and on

Pick your road and open it up.

By now you work in IT and are paid to learn. Year two is where you choose a specialty, your salary starts to climb, and remote work becomes realistic. The roads are below. If postings keep asking for a degree, WGU's online IT programs turn the certificates you already hold into college credit, on your schedule.

03 — Year two and beyond

Three roads. All of them pay. Pick what pulls you.

You do not have to decide today. By the time you get there, you will have worked around all three.

Defend networks for a living

CySA+SSCPlater · CISSP

The natural step after Security+. You would work a Security Operations Center, watching for and answering attacks, or serve as a security specialist on base. Strongest fit with a clearance: cleared cyber roles are among the best-paid jobs that require no degree, and the DoD shortage is permanent. Your Linux+ work pays off here; most security tooling lives on Linux.

SOC analyst roles are commonly remote in the US market.

Why AI feeds it

AI makes attacks cheaper and more frequent, which means more defense work, not less. Companies need a human accountable for security decisions. The tooling changes; the need compounds.

Run the infrastructure everything lives on

AWS Cloud PractitionerAWS Solutions Architector Azure AZ-104

Everything runs in the cloud now, the military included. Cloud administrators manage servers that live in distant data centers, which means the work is remote by nature. This is the most direct route to a work-from-anywhere career.

Taste it early with AWS Cloud Practitioner or Azure AZ-900, both beginner exams near $100.

Why AI feeds it

Every AI product runs on cloud infrastructure. The AI boom is, underneath, a cloud spending boom. Someone has to build, secure, and babysit all of it.

Be the person who makes AI actually work

started in first gearAzure AI-102 nextPython basics

You started this road in week one; that was the point of first gear. From here it becomes a multiplier on the other two. Learn to drive AI tools seriously, automate tasks, script with AI's help, and you become the technician who gets three times more done. Small businesses everywhere need the person who sets up AI for them, and almost nobody without a degree is competing for that yet.

The honest read

AI is consuming the boring half of IT: password resets, ticket triage. It is not consuming hands-on hardware work, security, or infrastructure. Position yourself on the winning side of that line from day one.

AI won't take your job. A person using AI will. So be that person.
