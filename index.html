01 — Your starting position

You hold three advantages most people don't.

This is not motivation-poster talk. These are structural advantages in the exact job market you are aiming at.

A—

You are a US citizen

Being Puerto Rican means citizenship, and citizenship means you are eligible for a security clearance. Most people on Earth never can be. Cleared IT workers earn more and get hired faster, because the clearance is often harder to find than the skill.

B—

You are already on base

You work inside a US military installation. The IT offices there, government and contractor alike, hire from people who are already local, already badged, already known. You can walk to the buildings that will employ you.

C—

DoD does not need your diploma

Department of Defense rules, known as DoD 8140, qualify people for IT work based on certifications, not degrees. A $400 exam you can pass with free training counts. The four-year degree is optional in this world.

The cheat code

CompTIA Security+ is the key that opens the base doors.

Nearly every IT job that touches a DoD network legally requires a baseline certification, and Security+ is the standard one. Help desk at the network center, contractor field tech, sysadmin support: they all list it. This whole roadmap points at Security+ by the end of year one. Once you hold it and can clear, you sit in a small pool of people that government contractors compete to hire.

02 — The route

One year. Five gears.

Each gear is a milestone with a clear finish line. Don't skip gears, and don't stay parked. The timeline assumes five to eight hours of study a week while you keep your job.

First gearMonths 0–2 · Free

Test drive: find out if you like this.

Before spending a cent, put in a few weeks and see if the work clicks.

  • Watch Professor Messer's free CompTIA A+ course on YouTube. It is the standard free training, and it is genuinely good.
  • Get hands-on. Open an old PC, reinstall Windows on something, break settings and repair them.
  • Optional paid shortcut: the Google IT Support Certificate on Coursera, about $49 a month, most finish inside five. A fine resume line, not a requirement.
Cost · $0
Second gearMonths 2–6

CompTIA A+, your first real certificate.

A+ proves you can support computers, printers, phones, and the people attached to them. It is the entry ticket for help desk work everywhere.

  • Two exams, Core 1 and Core 2, roughly $250+ each. Confirm current pricing at comptia.org.
  • Study with Messer, then drill practice exams. ExamCompass is free; Messer's paid sets are worth the money.
  • Apply to entry help desk jobs the day you pass. Not when you feel ready. Nobody ever feels ready.
Cost · ~$500 in exams
Third gearMonths 6–9 · Optional

CompTIA Network+, how it all connects.

Networking is the plumbing of IT. Understanding it makes every job after this one easier.

  • One exam, roughly $370.
  • Straight talk: if money is tight, skip to Security+. Network+ makes Security+ easier and reads well on a resume, but Security+ is the one employers require.
Cost · ~$370, skippable
Fourth gearMonths 9–12 · The big one

CompTIA Security+, the DoD door opener.

The certificate this whole plan is built around. One exam, roughly $400, and it satisfies the DoD 8140 baseline that base IT jobs demand.

  • Study Messer's free Security+ course, then practice exams until you hold 85 percent or better.
  • The day you pass: open a USAJOBS.gov account, search IT roles at your installation, and check the career pages of GDIT, Leidos, Vectrus, and KBR, all of whom staff bases in Germany.
  • If a job offers to sponsor your security clearance, take it, even at average pay. The clearance is worth more than the first salary.
Cost · ~$400
Fifth gearYear 2 and on

Pick a lane and open it up.

By now you work in IT and are paid to learn. Year two is where you choose a specialty, your salary starts to climb, and remote work becomes realistic. The lanes are below.

Employers often pay for these certs
03 — Year two and beyond

Three lanes. All of them pay. Pick what pulls you.

You do not have to decide today. By the time you get there, you will have worked around all three.

Defend networks for a living

CySA+SSCPlater · CISSP

The natural step after Security+. You would work a Security Operations Center, watching for and answering attacks, or serve as a security specialist on base. Strongest fit with a clearance: cleared cyber roles are among the best-paid jobs that require no degree, and the DoD shortage is permanent.

SOC analyst roles are commonly remote in the US market.

Why AI feeds it

AI makes attacks cheaper and more frequent, which means more defense work, not less. Companies need a human accountable for security decisions. The tooling changes; the need compounds.

Run the infrastructure everything lives on

AWS Cloud PractitionerAWS Solutions Architector Azure AZ-104

Everything runs in the cloud now, the military included. Cloud administrators manage servers that live in distant data centers, which means the work is remote by nature. This is the most direct route to a work-from-anywhere career.

Taste it early with AWS Cloud Practitioner or Azure AZ-900, both beginner exams near $100.

Why AI feeds it

Every AI product runs on cloud infrastructure. The AI boom is, underneath, a cloud spending boom. Someone has to build, secure, and babysit all of it.

Be the person who makes AI actually work

Azure AI-900Python basicsAI tool fluency

Less a certificate path than a multiplier on the other two. Learn to drive AI tools seriously, automate tasks, script with AI's help, and you become the technician who gets three times more done. Small businesses everywhere need the person who sets up AI for them, and almost nobody without a degree is competing for that yet.

The honest read

AI is consuming the boring half of IT: password resets, ticket triage. It is not consuming hands-on hardware work, security, or infrastructure. Position yourself on the winning side of that line from day one.

AI won't take your job. A person using AI will. So be that person.

06 — Ignition

Three moves this week.

1

Watch the first five Professor Messer A+ videos

Search "Professor Messer A+ 220-1201" on YouTube. It costs nothing. If you are still curious after five videos, this field is for you.

2

Open a USAJOBS.gov account and window-shop

Search IT jobs at your installation now, before you are qualified. Read the requirements. You will see Security+ everywhere, and you will see the finish line you are driving toward.

3

Talk to the IT people on base

You already work there. Find the people who fix the computers and ask how they got in, and which contractors are hiring. On base, jobs travel by word of mouth long before they get posted.
